Old Tappan vs. DePaul  ·  May 14, 2026
OLD TAPPAN 3  ·  DePAUL 2

Selden Kolkebeck was the story from first pitch to last, spinning a gem on the mound as Old Tappan (19-4) edged No. 11 DePaul (17-5) by a final of 3-2 in a tightly contested Big North showdown. Kolkebeck went the distance — all seven innings — allowing just three hits and two runs, walking two, and punching out an eye-popping ten batters on 99 pitches to improve his ERA to a pristine 1.00 on the season.

"Ten strikeouts. Seven innings. One earned run. Kolkebeck is pitching at a level few in the state can match right now."

— High School Sports Elite

Old Tappan scratched across single runs in the 2nd, 5th, and 6th innings, with Joey Crescitelli plating the go-ahead run and Anthony Onnembo reaching base three times on walks to keep rallies alive throughout the afternoon. It was methodical, patient, and exactly the kind of offense that complements an elite arm on the mound.

DePaul got a spark from speedy leadoff man Jason Amalbert, who reached base twice, scored both Cavalier runs, and swiped three bags to give his team every opportunity to steal the game. J.C. Pacheco delivered the big blow — a clutch single that drove in both DePaul runs in a late bid to claw back into contention. But it wasn't enough. DePaul starter Jackson Schessler surrendered seven walks in 5.2 innings, and three costly Cavalier errors gave Old Tappan just enough breathing room to hold on.

When Kolkebeck retired the final batter to seal it, the Golden Knights had knocked off a top-15 program and served notice that they are a team playing their best baseball at exactly the right time of year. With a 19-4 record and a 9-1 mark in the Big North National division, Old Tappan is a serious postseason contender — and Kolkebeck is their ace, their anchor, and their best argument for a deep playoff run.
